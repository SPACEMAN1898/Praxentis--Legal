ClinikChat — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 24 April 2026  ·  Praxentis / Spine Tech AU

Plain-language summary: ClinikChat is a secure clinical messaging app for registered healthcare practitioners and their patients in Australia. Your message content is protected by end-to-end encryption — we cannot read it. We collect only what is necessary to provide the service and comply with Australian law. This policy explains exactly what we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

1. Who we are

ClinikChat is operated by Praxentis (trading as Spine Tech AU). We built ClinikChat to enable secure, AHPRA-compliant clinical communication between healthcare practitioners and their patients in Australia.

For all privacy enquiries, contact: owner@praxentis.co.site

2. Scope of this policy

This policy applies to all users of ClinikChat, including:

  • Registered healthcare practitioners (practitioners) using the app to communicate with patients
  • Patients who receive and respond to messages through ClinikChat
  • Clinic administrators who manage practitioner accounts

It covers data collected through the ClinikChat mobile application, any associated web interface, and our backend infrastructure.

3. Data we collect

3.1 Account information

When a practitioner or patient account is created, we collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Mobile phone number (for account verification and notifications)
  • For practitioners: AHPRA registration number and profession type
  • For clinics: practice name and ABN (where applicable)
  • Profile photo (optional)

3.2 Message and communication data

ClinikChat uses end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for all message content. This means:

  • Message text, images, attachments, and files are encrypted on your device before transmission
  • Only the intended recipient(s) can decrypt and read message content
  • Praxentis and Spine Tech AU cannot access, read, or decrypt your message content

We do collect non-content metadata necessary to route messages, including: sender and recipient identifiers, message timestamps, delivery status, and message size. This metadata is not encrypted but is access-controlled and treated as sensitive.

3.3 Health information

ClinikChat is designed for clinical communication. Messages may contain health information as defined under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). Because message content is end-to-end encrypted, Praxentis does not hold, access, or process that health information — it remains under the control of the practitioner and patient parties to the conversation.

Where a practitioner shares documents or clinical files through ClinikChat (for example, referral letters or test results), those files are encrypted in transit and at rest. Access is restricted to the conversation participants.

3.4 Technical and usage data

We collect limited technical data to operate and improve the service:

  • Device type and operating system version
  • App version
  • IP address (used for fraud detection and rate limiting; not linked to message content)
  • Crash reports and error logs (which do not include message content)
  • Session timestamps (login/logout events)

4. How we use your data

We use the data we collect to:

  • Provide and operate the ClinikChat messaging service
  • Verify practitioner registration against AHPRA records
  • Authenticate users and prevent unauthorised access
  • Deliver push notifications for new messages (notification content is minimal and does not reveal message body)
  • Detect and prevent abuse, fraud, and security threats
  • Comply with our legal obligations under Australian law
  • Respond to privacy requests, complaints, and enquiries
  • Improve the reliability and performance of the service (using aggregate, de-identified data)

We do not use your data for advertising, sell it to third parties, or use it for any purpose unrelated to operating ClinikChat.

5. End-to-end encryption

ClinikChat implements end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for all clinical messages. The encryption keys are generated and stored on your device. When you send a message:

  • Your device encrypts the message using the recipient's public key
  • The encrypted message is transmitted to our servers
  • Our servers relay the encrypted message to the recipient's device
  • Only the recipient's device, using their private key, can decrypt and read the message

This architecture means that even if our servers were compromised, your message content would remain unreadable to any third party. The encryption applies to text, images, documents, and all other file types sent through the app.

6. AHPRA compliance

ClinikChat is designed to support practitioners in meeting their obligations under the Health Practitioner Regulation National Law. Specifically:

  • Practitioner accounts are verified against the AHPRA register before clinical messaging features are enabled
  • Only practitioners with a current, active AHPRA registration may use the clinical practitioner features
  • The app is not intended for use by practitioners whose registration is suspended, cancelled, or lapsed
  • Practitioners remain individually responsible for their professional conduct within ClinikChat, including the appropriateness of clinical communications, in accordance with their board's guidelines

7. Australian Privacy Act 1988 compliance

Praxentis is bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). In particular:

  • APP 1 (Open and transparent management): This policy sets out our privacy practices clearly and is publicly available.
  • APP 3 (Collection of solicited personal information): We collect only personal information that is reasonably necessary for our functions. Health information is collected only where the user has provided express consent.
  • APP 5 (Notification of collection): We notify users of the purpose of data collection at the time of account creation and in this policy.
  • APP 6 (Use and disclosure): We use personal information only for the primary purpose for which it was collected, or where the user has consented to a secondary use.
  • APP 11 (Security): We take reasonable steps to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, including through E2EE, encrypted storage, and access controls.
  • APP 12 (Access to personal information): Individuals may request access to their personal information. See Section 11 below.
  • APP 13 (Correction of personal information): Individuals may request correction of inaccurate personal information. See Section 11 below.

Health information accessed or communicated through ClinikChat is sensitive information under the Privacy Act. We treat it accordingly and collect it only with the user's consent or as permitted by law.

8. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide the service and meet our legal obligations:

  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your account and for 7 years after account closure, in accordance with Australian health records legislation and tax obligations.
  • Message metadata: Retained for 7 years to support clinical record-keeping obligations applicable to practitioners.
  • Encrypted message content: Stored on our servers in encrypted form for as long as the conversation is active. Users may delete individual messages or entire conversations at any time; deleted content is removed from our servers within 30 days.
  • Technical/usage logs: Retained for a maximum of 12 months, then deleted or anonymised.

When an account is permanently deleted, we will delete or anonymise associated personal information, except where retention is required by law.

9. Disclosure of personal information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may disclose personal information to:

  • Third-party service providers who assist us in operating ClinikChat (for example, cloud hosting providers and push notification services). These providers are contractually bound to process data only as directed by us and to maintain appropriate security.
  • AHPRA, where required to verify practitioner registration status.
  • Law enforcement or government agencies, where required by law or a valid court order. We will notify affected users of such disclosures where legally permitted to do so.
  • Successors in interest, in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of our business, subject to the successor maintaining equivalent privacy protections.

We will never disclose message content because we do not have the ability to access it — it is end-to-end encrypted and only decryptable by conversation participants.

10. Data storage and security

Personal information is stored on servers located in Australia. We implement the following security measures:

  • End-to-end encryption for all message content
  • Encryption at rest for all stored data (AES-256 or equivalent)
  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) for all API communications
  • Role-based access controls limiting staff access to personal information
  • Multi-factor authentication for all administrative access
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Audit logging of access to personal information

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act.

11. Your rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request that inaccurate, incomplete, or out-of-date information be corrected.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated personal information, subject to our legal retention obligations.
  • Complaint: Lodge a complaint with us about how we have handled your personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at owner@praxentis.co.site. We will respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.

12. Children

ClinikChat is intended for use by registered healthcare practitioners (who must be adults) and patients who are 18 years of age or older. The app is not directed at children under 18. If a patient under 18 is to use ClinikChat, a parent or legal guardian must provide consent and manage the account on their behalf. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18 without appropriate parental consent.

13. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date above and notify users via in-app notification and, where practical, by email. Continued use of ClinikChat after changes to this policy constitutes acceptance of the updated terms. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
